### Twitter/X — single tweet

> Chemistry teachers: your periodic table wall chart just got a free
> upgrade. 🧪
>
> Elements is an open-source PWA — 118 sourced element stories, 5 games,
> 12 safe experiments, and a teacher dashboard that shows every student's
> progress. Self-hostable on free infrastructure.
>
> 🔗

### Twitter/X — thread (6 tweets)

1. Schools: what if the periodic table were the most-requested screen in
   your classroom? A free, open-source tool for grades 3–7. 🧵

2. Elements turns all 118 elements into a paced, self-study journey —
   25 unlockable chapters, 5 learning games, 12 safety-gated experiments.
   Every fact is sourced (IUPAC/CRC referenced) and every metaphor is
   labeled as one. Data file citations are in the repo.

3. New: an institution dashboard. Add students by school email, then see
   at a glance: who's active this week, elements met per student, badges
   earned, game progress. No grading — just visibility.

4. Privacy by design: kids never need accounts. Sign-in (Google
   Workspace) is optional, school-managed, and add-only by an admin.
   Progress lives on the device first, cloud second. No ads, no trackers.

5. Deployment: your IT person can run the whole thing on Render's free
   tier + MongoDB Atlas free tier in under an hour — the repo ships with
   a one-click blueprint and step-by-step docs. Works offline on school
   iPads once loaded.

6. Open source. Inspect every fact, every line. Try it, fork it, tell us
   what your students think:
   🔗 app · 🔗 repo · 🔗 deploy guide
